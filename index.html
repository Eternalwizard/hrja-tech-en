EPA 30B Sampling Tube:

Sampling method: 30B mercury adsorption tube is a special sampling tube for method 30B, which meets the requirements of US EPA Method 30B. It is divided into three types: total gas mercury adsorption tube, gas phase mercury adsorption tube, and spiked adsorption tube. It uses modified activated carbon and is installed at the front end of the heated sampling probe of the gas mercury sampler to absorb and collect gaseous mercury in the flue gas. The 30B mercury adsorption tube is used for flue gas mercury sampling from fixed pollution sources.

Sampling tube parameters: 185*10 mm;

Technical features:

  • Two-section adsorption tube with modified coconut shell activated carbon filling, quartz fiber cotton partition
  • Extremely low mercury content in modified activated carbon, high adsorption capacity for mercury, high collection efficiency, and not easy to penetrate due to high mercury background
  • Each tube has a unique number, facilitating the control of the sampling and analysis process
  • Support for customized spiked adsorption tubes with spiking error within ±3%

Mercury Testing Service: PYRO-915M (LUMEX)

Test samples: Mercury (Hg) content in solid, liquid, and gas samples;

Test standards:

  • "Environmental air determination of gaseous mercury portable cold atomic absorption spectrophotometry (trial)"
  • "Soil determination of total mercury portable cold atomic absorption spectrophotometry (trial)"
  • "GB 7468-1987 Water quality determination of total mercury cold atomic absorption spectrophotometry"
