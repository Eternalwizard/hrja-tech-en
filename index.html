EPA 30B Sampling Tubes:

Sampling method: 30B mercury adsorption tubes are specialized sampling tubes for method 30B, meeting US EPA Method 30B requirements. They are divided into three types: total mercury adsorption tubes, elemental mercury adsorption tubes, and spiked adsorption tubes. These tubes use modified activated carbon, installed at the front end of the heated sampling probe of the mercury sampling instrument, to adsorb and capture gaseous mercury in the flue gas. The 30B mercury adsorption tubes are used for flue gas mercury sampling from fixed pollution sources.

Sampling tube parameters: 185*10 mm;

Technical features:

  • Two-section adsorption tube, filled with modified coconut shell activated carbon, separated by quartz fiber cotton
  • Modified activated carbon has extremely low mercury content, large mercury adsorption capacity, and high collection efficiency, making it difficult to penetrate due to high mercury background
  • Each tube has a unique number, making it easy to control the sampling and analysis process
  • Supports customized spiked adsorption tubes with a spiking error within ±3%

Mercury Testing Services: PYRO-915M (LUMEX)

Test samples: Mercury (Hg) content in solid, liquid, and gas samples;

Test standards:

  • "Determination of Gaseous Mercury in Ambient Air by Portable Cold Vapor Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometry (Trial)"
  • "Determination of Total Mercury in Soil by Portable Cold Vapor Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometry (Trial)"
  • "GB 7468-1987 Determination of Total Mercury in Water Quality by Cold Vapor Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometry"
  • "HJ 543-2009 Determination of Mercury in Waste Gas from Fixed Pollution Sources by Cold Vapor Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometry"
  • "GB 15555.1-1995 Determination of Total Mercury in Solid Waste by Cold Vapor Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometry"
  • "EPA-7473 Mercury in Solids and Solutions by Thermal Decomposition Amalgamation and Atomic Absorption Spectrophotometry"
  • "EPA-30B Determination of Total Vapor Phase Mercury Emissions from Coal-Fired Combustion Sources Using Carbon Sorbent Traps"
